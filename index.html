Research & Publications

Manuscript in Preparation

Resolving Model Conflict with Adaptive Consensus Intelligence (ACI): A GNN-Based Framework for Flood Susceptibility Mapping

Abstract: Conventional flood susceptibility mapping often relies on single models or static ensembles that fail to resolve inter-model conflicts. This research introduces Adaptive Consensus Intelligence (ACI), which treats inter-model disagreement as a signal for conflict resolution. Using an ensemble of five expert models (U-Net, CatBoost, Physics-Informed Neural Network, objective MCDA, and Vision Transformer) on Jessore, Bangladesh, a Hydrological Graph Neural Network (GNN) is trained on raw data, base predictions, and disagreement signatures.

Key Innovation: The framework generates high-accuracy susceptibility maps, predictive uncertainty maps, and spatially-explainable "Model Trust Maps," advancing accurate, transparent, and physically coherent flood modeling.

Manuscript in Preparation

Tidal Flood Vulnerability Assessment in Satkhira, Bangladesh: A Multi-Criteria Approach Using Analytical Hierarchy Process and Remote Sensing Data

Abstract: Satkhira, a coastal region of Bangladesh, faces significant challenges from tidal flooding. This study developed a comprehensive flood-risk assessment framework using the Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP) integrated with GIS technology. Ten critical flood-influencing factors were systematically analyzed, weighted, and combined to generate a detailed composite flood risk map.

Key Results: The locally-adjusted model demonstrates superior performance compared to literature-based approaches, showing improved ROC-AUC, accuracy, precision, and F1-score metrics. The model excels particularly in classifying non-flooded areas and exhibits a clear positive correlation between risk categories and actual flood occurrence patterns.
